My path and story.

I did my undergraduate in economics at the Universidad de Piura. In my last two years as a student I had the opportunity to work as research assistant of Martin Paredes (Ph.D. Economics, NYU) and Miguel Angel Martínez (Ph.D. Business/Managerial Economics, Pompeu Fabra) in the fields of industrial organization, game theory, organizational and managerial economics in the Research Center of the University. Later, I also had the opportunity to work with Martín Valdivia (Ph.D. Applied Economics) on an impact evaluation (RCT) in the field of microfinance and women empowerment.

The last experience strongly motivated me to start a career in the Public Sector. I had the opportunity to work in the Strategic Planning Office of the Ministry of Education in Peru, where I was responsible for the analysis of indicators with emphasis on budget spending for Lima City. Lima concentrates more than 30% of the peruvian students, therefore, the impact of the successful delivery of the education service at this central level is crucial to achieve higher education outcomes as a country. After that, I joined the Ministry of Economics and Finance as an analyst at the National Budget Office, specifically with an emphasis in the subnational level where I had the opportunity to optimize many processes through the automatization of rutinary tasks with different kinds of tools such as: i) Stata, ii) Python, iii) R and iv) VBA.

This experience let me know better about how the subnational level entities coordinated with the central level and a better understanding of the public budget. Because of this, I was requested to lead the design of a multisectorial program, Early Childhood Development, that had to be coordinated with more than 10 Ministries, each with their own technical teams. The design was an innovative process where I was part of the formulation of the laws about Programas Preuspuestales Orientados a Resultados, and later its design. Within the same field of Early Childhood Development, we worked on an impact evaluation over anemia with administrative data; which was the first one of the Office developed inhouse.

Becoming the Data Center Leader in the Ministry of Economy and Finance was a challenge because as a young professional, from the career of economics, I was assuming the leadership on Data Governance and Open Data which implied frequent coordination with the Infrastructure and Technology Office. For this challenge I was supported by a very talented team of 10 people who were from different fields such as system engineering, statistics, computer science, economics, graphic design and marketing.

From my experience in the Strategic Planning Office of the Ministry of Education and the National Budget Office of the Ministry of Economy and Finance, I realized that there is a necessity of centralized data and advanced analysis skills for public policy decisions. Therefore, I was part of promoting the creation of a new Office of Information Management that watches over Data Governance, Open Data, Business Intelligence, System Development and Data Culture in the Ministry of Economy of Finance. After the creation, I became the Data Center leader and Talent Management leader who was in charge of the Data Governance and Open Data strategies, and culture and talent strategies; and gave support to the Analytics, BI and System Development teams, and other offices within the Ministry, with the objective to move towards to a data-driven institution.

With this Office recently implemented, during the 2020-2021 sanitary emergency in Peru I collaborated on many data based solutions that were important for reducing the number of infected and dead people caused by the Covid-19. In particular, I have participated in the definition of the process and the system called SISCOVID, coordinated with the Ministry of Health; developing a monitoring tool to control the agglomeration through hundreds of millions of data from the telephone operators (national level) and security video camera (district level); monitoring of the Personal Protective Equipment and Covid-19 vaccine acquisition and distribution

The access to data is necessary in many levels for decision takers in public policy, and the sanitary emergency made it clear. Therefore, with the objective of delivering the data and information and aligned with the national digital transformation plan, considering technical and not-technical profiles, I was in charge of planning and leading the development of three prioritized products. At a strategic level, I have promoted and have led the improvement and development of three portals in the Ministry of Economics and Finance: i) open data portal, ii) financial administration transparency portal and iii) information management tool. These three portals needed coordination at business, graphic design, user experience and interface, architecture, infrastructure, technology and web development level.

In all my of my jobs, I have worked with many programming languages and softwares such as: Python for data engineering and data analytics; Stata for statistics and impact evaluation; Matlab for simulation of theoretical models; PL/SQL for feature engineering; HTML, CSS and JS for web development (front-end and back-end); Q-Gis for spatial data analysis; and Tableau for data visualization. All of these tools were the support for my business skills so firstly I use problem solving frameworks to propose what is the hypothesis of the problem and possible solutions, for later analysis with the tools and translation to an executive view regularly through automated reports or dashboards. All of these are complemented with the use of project management solutions such as wrike or trello, and sometimes even a google sheet that would do the work to keep track of the tasks my team and I got to do.
